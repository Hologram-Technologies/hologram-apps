Native video content — content-addressed media for the Video holospace (Law L5).

Every file here is pinned by sha256 in video-manifest.json; video.html re-derives the
default source before playing it, and video-witness.mjs fails on any drift. This is the
"100% UOR content-addressable" default content: the bytes the holospace plays ARE their
own address. The bundled clip and the HLS ladder carry an AUDIO track (AAC stereo), so
"audio works for every video" is testable offline. For remote 4K / Low-Latency-HLS
streaming, open video.html?src=<url> — the adaptive ladder is then addressed by its HLS
playlist, not bundled here (the verified 4K and 1080p remote streams also carry audio).

cmaf/ — ONE content-addressed CMAF (fragmented-MP4) segment set, described by BOTH an
        HLS playlist (master.m3u8, #EXT-X-VERSION:7) AND an MPEG-DASH manifest
        (stream.mpd). Two open standards, identical κ-segments: init-stream{0,1,2}.m4s +
        chunk-stream{0,1,2}-*.m4s (reps 0/1 = 640×360 + 480×270 video, rep 2 = AAC audio).
        Built from the MP4 above with ffmpeg's DASH muxer (-hls_playlist). Each segment is
        independently content-addressed; served through holo:// every .m4s is a verified
        κ-object reused by HLS and DASH alike (max-UOR dedup across protocols).
